# The Healing Ground Geneva: Brand, Website, SEO, and Launch Strategy

## 1. Brand Strategy

Mission: help internationally minded adults in Geneva rebuild nervous-system safety, emotional steadiness, and embodied self-trust through trauma-informed coaching.

Vision: a Geneva wellness culture where burnout, sensitivity, stress, and persistent symptoms are met with nuance, dignity, and grounded care.

Values: safety before insight; consent and pacing; quiet intelligence; cultural sensitivity; body respect; ethical boundaries; emotional precision; premium simplicity; non-performative compassion.

Personality: calm, discerning, warm, grounded, editorial, emotionally literate, discreet, culturally fluent.

Archetypes: primary caregiver, secondary sage, tertiary innocent in the sense of return-to-simplicity rather than naivete.

Emotional positioning: a calm exhale for high-functioning people whose bodies are asking for a slower way to live.

Voice: refined but human; soft but clear; never grandiose; never clinical-cold; never mystical. Use plain language with emotional specificity.

Messaging hierarchy:
1. Come home to your body.
2. Trauma-informed coaching for stress, burnout, nervous-system dysregulation, emotional overwhelm, and persistent stress-related symptoms.
3. Designed for expats, UN/NGO professionals, diplomats, and internationally mobile adults in Geneva.
4. Grounded support with ethical boundaries: coaching, not diagnosis or medical treatment.

Elevator pitch: The Healing Ground Geneva is a trauma-informed coaching practice led by Celia de Mestral, supporting expats and international professionals with stress, burnout, nervous-system dysregulation, emotional overwhelm, and persistent stress-related symptoms.

Founder story framework: Celia saw a need for support that was emotionally deep without being dramatic, sophisticated without being cold, and body-aware without becoming pseudo-medical. Her work offers a calm container for people living under sustained pressure to understand what their system has been carrying and what it may need next.

Manifesto: We believe the body is not an obstacle to productivity. It is a source of information. We believe healing language should be careful. We believe safety cannot be rushed. We believe internationally mobile people deserve support that understands adaptation, belonging, discretion, and invisible stress. We believe coming home to your body is not a performance; it is a quiet return.

Power words: grounded, steady, gentle, spacious, attuned, capacity, safety, choice, belonging, rhythm, integration, listening, relief, return, clarity.

Avoid: cure, fix, unlock, transform overnight, feminine energy, quantum, manifest, biohack, trauma release guarantee, root cause cure, high vibe, goddess, hustle, breakthrough.

Trauma-informed communication rules: ask rather than assume; avoid urgency pressure; name choice; avoid graphic trauma language; avoid before/after transformation claims; validate protective responses; use disclaimers calmly; make opt-outs visible.

Psychological trust principles: clear ethical boundaries; transparent process; low-pressure CTAs; local context; multilingual sensitivity; calm design; testimonials focused on felt experience rather than miracle claims.

Geneva differentiation: most wellness offers in Geneva skew clinical, spa-luxury, yoga-spiritual, or corporate resilience. The Healing Ground Geneva occupies the editorial middle: emotionally intelligent, trauma-informed, expat-aware, premium, and non-mystical.

UVP: Trauma-informed coaching in Geneva for internationally intense lives.

## 2. Visual Identity

Palette:
- Ink `#223029`: primary text, buttons, footer.
- Cedar `#3D5047`: hover, secondary grounding.
- Sage `#8C9A85`: soft trust, accents.
- Clay `#A26F5F`: human warmth, restrained emphasis.
- Stone `#8E8A80`: quiet secondary text.
- Mist `#EDE9DF`: section background.
- Oat `#D9D0C1`: dividers and muted surfaces.
- Ivory `#FBF8F0`: primary background.

Typography: elegant serif for display headlines; sober sans for body and UI. Current implementation uses CSS variable stacks to avoid build-time font fetching. Recommended production pairing: Canela or Cormorant Garamond for display; Suisse Int'l, Inter, or Neue Haas Grotesk for UI.

Hierarchy: H1 48-102px fluid via clamp; H2 38-75px; H3 24-36px; body 18-20px on key pages; UI 14px semibold; eyebrow uppercase with generous tracking.

Photography: natural daylight, Geneva-adjacent calm, notebooks, quiet rooms, lake/stone/water, hands, architectural stillness, never hyper-feminine wellness poses. Avoid lotus stock images, crystals, sage smoke, beach cliches, influencer portraits.

Image prompts: "Scandinavian editorial wellness consultation room, soft Geneva daylight, warm stone, quiet notebook, minimal, premium, human, no people posing"; "Lake Geneva morning, soft grey-green palette, quiet luxury wellness brand, editorial photography".

UI: 8px radius cards, full-width bands, restrained shadows, high whitespace, no decorative blobs, no nested cards, CTA pills, clear focus states.

Spacing: base 4px scale; sections 80-112px vertical; cards 24-32px padding; grids 20-48px gaps.

Motion: minimal; use slow opacity/translate only if added; respect prefers-reduced-motion. No parallax or restless animation.

Accessibility: WCAG AA contrast, visible focus, semantic headings, alt text for meaningful images, buttons/links as native elements, 44px touch targets, no text over low-contrast imagery without overlay.

## 11. Discovery Call Funnel

Flow: low-pressure CTA -> short form -> confirmation with reassurance -> reminder -> call -> recap and next step.

Intake questions: What would you like support with? What has felt hardest recently? Are you currently working with a therapist/doctor? Are there any access needs or pacing preferences? Preferred language? Are you in immediate crisis? What would make this conversation feel safe enough?

Safety principles: explicit choice, no pressure to disclose, referral when outside scope, slow pacing, clear confidentiality limits.

Confirmation email: "Thank you for reaching out. You do not need to prepare a perfect story. Bring only what feels relevant."

Post-call sequence: recap fit; offer plan; provide referral if not fit; 5-day gentle check-in; workshop/newsletter invitation.

Booking: Cal.com, TidyCal, or Calendly embedded after launch. Keep contact form as backup.

Newsletter: Resend Broadcasts or ConvertKit/Beehiiv if creator-style growth becomes central.

Analytics: PostHog EU; Search Console; Vercel Analytics optional.

SEO tools: Google Search Console, Google Business Profile, Ahrefs/SEMrush optional, Screaming Frog for audits.

CRM: Airtable or Notion CRM first; HubSpot only if corporate workshops grow.

Automation: Resend welcome sequence, Zapier/Make for form -> CRM -> email, PostHog conversion events.
