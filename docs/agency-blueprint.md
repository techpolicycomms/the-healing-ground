# The Healing Ground Geneva: Complete Digital Presence Blueprint

This document is the launch blueprint for The Healing Ground Geneva: brand strategy, identity system, website architecture, premium copy, multilingual direction, SEO, content, Instagram, newsletter, workshops, discovery-call funnel, compliance, Geneva expat strategy, tech stack, and launch plan.

## Section 1: Complete Brand Strategy

### Strategic Role

The Healing Ground Geneva should occupy a rare position in the Geneva wellness market: emotionally intelligent and trauma-informed, but not clinical-cold; premium and refined, but not elitist; body-aware, but not mystical; culturally sensitive, but not vague.

The brand is for people who have learned to function beautifully under pressure and now need a place where their body, emotions, and exhaustion can be taken seriously without being pathologised.

### Mission

To offer calm, trauma-informed coaching that helps internationally minded adults in Geneva rebuild safety, capacity, emotional steadiness, and embodied self-trust in the face of chronic stress, burnout, overwhelm, and persistent stress-related symptoms.

### Vision

A Geneva wellness culture where high-functioning people no longer have to wait for collapse before receiving nuanced support, and where sensitivity, stress, burnout, and body signals are met with dignity, cultural intelligence, and grounded care.

### Brand Values

Safety before insight: the work never rushes a person into disclosure, catharsis, or performance. A regulated container matters more than dramatic breakthroughs.

Consent and choice: clients are invited, not pushed. The brand language consistently reinforces agency, pacing, and opt-in participation.

Grounded compassion: warmth without sentimentality; care without overpromising; kindness with clean boundaries.

Quiet intelligence: thoughtful, evidence-aware, precise language. No inflated claims, no trend-chasing, no spiritual theatre.

Cultural sensitivity: Geneva is an international city. The brand must understand language-switching, mobility, discretion, belonging, status pressure, institutional pressure, and cross-cultural identity.

Body respect: the body is treated as a source of information, not an enemy to control or a machine to optimise.

Ethical clarity: coaching is not therapy, emergency care, medical diagnosis, or medical treatment. The brand earns trust by naming this clearly.

Premium simplicity: refined materials, restrained design, calm white space, careful words, and an atmosphere of ease.

### Brand Archetypes

Primary archetype: The Caregiver. The brand offers support, protection, attunement, and calm presence.

Secondary archetype: The Sage. The brand educates gently and helps people make sense of their nervous system without overwhelm.

Tertiary archetype: The Everyperson. The tone remains accessible and non-elitist, even while the visual system feels premium.

Avoid over-indexing on The Magician, The Lover, or The Hero. Those would pull the brand toward transformation fantasy, sensual luxury, or achievement language.

## Section 2: Full Visual Identity System

### Design Strategy

The design should communicate safety before the visitor reads a sentence. It should feel slow, spacious, editorial, grounded, and quietly premium. The site should borrow from Scandinavian editorial wellness, Aesop-like restraint, Swiss spacing discipline, gentle therapy practices, and modern retreat branding.

### Color Palette

Primary:
- Ink `#223029`: deep green-black. Use for primary text, footer, primary CTA.
- Ivory `#FBF8F0`: warm off-white. Use as the main background.

Secondary:
- Cedar `#3D5047`: grounded green. Use for hover states, secondary emphasis.
- Sage `#8C9A85`: muted grey-green. Use for soft accents and calm highlights.
- Mist `#EDE9DF`: warm grey-beige. Use for section bands.

Human warmth:
- Clay `#A26F5F`: muted terracotta. Use for eyebrows, small accents, warmth.
- Oat `#D9D0C1`: quiet neutral. Use for borders, cards, muted surfaces.
- Stone `#8E8A80`: secondary text and subtle UI.

Usage ratio:
- 55% Ivory
- 20% Mist/Oat
- 15% Ink/Cedar
- 10% Clay/Sage accents

Avoid a one-note beige theme by letting Ink and Cedar provide structure and Clay provide human warmth.

### Typography System

Production recommendation:
- Display serif: Canela, Cormorant Garamond, or Editorial New.
- Sans: Suisse Int'l, Neue Haas Grotesk, Inter, or Untitled Sans.

Current implementation:
- Uses robust system stacks via Tailwind font families to avoid external font build risk.

Hierarchy:
- H1: 48-102px, serif, line-height 0.92-1.0, weight 600.
- H2: 38-75px, serif, line-height 0.95-1.05.
- H3: 24-36px, sans or serif depending section.
- Body large: 18-20px, line-height 1.7.
- Body standard: 16px, line-height 1.65.
- UI labels: 14px, semibold.
- Eyebrows: 12-14px, uppercase, tracking 0.20-0.24em.

Never use viewport-only font scaling. Use `clamp()`.

Use:

- Editorial crops with space

Avoid:
- Yoga poses as hero imagery
- Crystals, incense, smoke, sound bowls
- Overexposed influencer portraits
- Stock images of people looking dramatically distressed
- Clinical rooms that feel cold

Moodboard prompts:

### Illustration Direction

Use almost no illustration. If used:
- Thin-line abstract topographic contours
- Soft breathing-rhythm linework
- Minimal botanical line detail
- Small schematic nervous-system education diagrams

Avoid mystical symbols, mandalas, chakra diagrams, moons/stars as primary identity, and decorative blobs.

### UI Aesthetic

Buttons:
- Primary: Ink background, Ivory text, rounded-full, min-height 48px, 24-28px horizontal padding.
- Hover: Cedar background.
- Secondary: transparent/Ivory with Stone border, Ink text.
- Text link: Cedar text, underline on hover.

Cards:
- 8px radius maximum.
- 1px Stone/Oat border.
- Soft shadow only when useful.
- No nested cards.

Sections:
- Full-width bands in Ivory, Mist, or Ink.
- Max-width 7xl inner container.
- No floating decorative cards as entire sections.

### Spacing System

Base unit: 4px.

Vertical rhythm:
- Section mobile: 80px.
- Section desktop: 112px.
- Component gaps: 20-48px.
- Card padding: 24px mobile, 32px desktop.
- Header padding: 16px vertical, 20-32px horizontal.

### Motion Guidelines

Motion should feel like a slow breath, not a performance.

Allowed:
- Subtle opacity fade
- 4-8px translate
- 200-400ms easing

Avoid:
- Parallax
- Scroll hijacking
- Constant animated backgrounds
- Bouncy transitions
- Excessive hover transforms

Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.

### Accessibility Guidelines

- Minimum text contrast 4.5:1.
- Large text 3:1 minimum.
- Touch targets at least 44x44px.
- Visible focus states.
- One H1 per page.
- Semantic landmarks.
- Form labels always visible.
- Error states with `aria-live`.
- Decorative images get empty alt.
- Meaningful images get concise alt.

### Mobile-First Direction

Mobile should feel composed, not like a compressed desktop.

Rules:
- Single-column sections.
- CTA buttons full-width or near full-width where helpful.
- Header brand may wrap elegantly.
- Avoid dense nav on mobile; primary discovery call remains visible.
- Do not use tiny typography in cards.
- Preserve generous vertical breathing room.

### FAQ Copy

Is this therapy?
No. This is trauma-informed coaching. It can be emotionally deep and body-aware, but it does not diagnose or treat mental health conditions. If therapy or medical care is more appropriate, Celia will say so.

Do I need to talk about trauma?
No. Trauma-informed means the work respects safety, consent, pacing, choice, and the body's protective responses. You never have to disclose more than feels right.

Who is this for?
The work is especially relevant for expats, internationally mobile adults, UN and NGO professionals, diplomats, and high-functioning people who feel exhausted, overwhelmed, or disconnected from themselves.

Can sessions happen online?
Yes. Sessions can take place online and are designed to feel calm, structured, and private. Geneva-based in-person formats may be offered for workshops and selected sessions.

Which languages are available?
English is the primary website language. French and Spanish are welcome in the brand ecosystem and can be used where appropriate depending on the session or format.

Will this cure my symptoms?
No ethical practitioner can promise that. This work supports nervous-system capacity, stress awareness, emotional regulation, and embodied self-trust. It should sit alongside appropriate medical or therapeutic care when needed.
